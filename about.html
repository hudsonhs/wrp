Mission Statement

This student organization was started at Wesleyan University in the Fall of 2015. We are a passionate group of Wesleyan students dedicated to volunteering, advocating, fundraising, and raising awareness of current refugee crises. We also work with a number of international and local nonprofit organizations, assisting in areas such as tutoring, legal aid, and refugee resettlement. We support primarily grassroots and community-based organizations, pairing local engagement with global advocacy. Visit and like our Facebook page to learn more about us and keep up on our events and future happenings.

About Casey

Casey is a senior at Wesleyan and the President of WRP. She is a College of Social Studies major pursuing certificates in Middle Eastern Studies and International Relations. After working as an intern with Integrated Refugee and Immigrant Services and the International Refugee Assistance Project, Casey wanted more Wesleyan students to get involved with these and other refugee-related organizations, so she collaborated with Cole and Sophie to start WRP in Fall 2015.

Casey worked at the U.S. Department of State in the Refugee Admissions Office in 2016 and is working on a thesis about aid policy in Jordan. She sees U.S. aid and refugee resettlement programs as a vital way to improve intercultural understanding between the U.S. and the Middle East, and intends to pursue a career in government supporting these programs.

About Caroline

Caroline is the fundraising and financial manager for WRP and also volunteers with IRAP. She loves event-planning and collaborating with Middletown residents, student groups, and other nonprofit organizations dedicated to providing refugees with long-term support.

Caroline is a sophomore majoring in history and pursuing a certificate in civic engagement. She is from New York City.

About Cheryl

Cheryl is a senior majoring in Religion and also receiving Certificates in Middle Eastern Studies and International Relations. She currently manages Paper Airplanes which is an English tutoring program that remotely connects American college students with Syrian Refugee students who are living all over the world. Cheryl plans on continuing her work with refugees when she graduates.

About Julia

Julia has been involved with several aspects of WRP over the past year. She mostly spends her time with IRIS, the Integrated Refugee and Immigrant Services in New Haven, for which she is the Direct Service Coordinator. Currently, the IRIS/WRP partnership involves 12 Wesleyan students traveling weekly to New Haven to help refugees through the process of filling out and submitting Energy Assistance Applications.

On campus, Julia is double-majoring in Anthropology and Feminist, Gender, and Sexuality Studies. She is also involved in several other projects, including a sexual health awareness and education community service program, an annual body-positive dance show fundraiser for Planned Parenthood, and an a cappella group!

About Helena

Helena is a fifth-year student at Wesleyan obtaining a master's degree in Molecular Biology and Biochemistry (MB&B). She graduated with a B.A. in 2016, having double majored in Chemistry and MB&B with a certificate in Molecular Biophysics. Within WRP, Helena coordinates the International Refugee Assistance Project (IRAP).

About Sahar

Sahar is a senior at Wesleyan and is majoring in Government with a concentration in Comparative Politics. This year Sahar is helping lead WRP's Civic Engagement and Advocacy team. This year Sahar is looking forward to engaging in dialogue with Wesleyan and Middletown and learning more about the Connecticut refugee community. This semester Sahar will also be acting as the education intern for IRIS (Integrated Refugee and Immigrant Services) and is looking forward to learning more about resettlement process and the journey through education for refugees.

About Libby

Libby is a prospective Science in Society major, with a minor in East Asian Studies. As one of the current leaders of the Advocacy and Civic Engagement team for WRP, she is looking forward to working with Wesleyan students and surrounding communities to raise awareness of and take action towards the ongoing refugee crisis through a variety of means such as Art installations, visiting speakers, fundraising partnerships, and more. Last year she was involved in several WRP endeavors, including tutoring through Paper Airplanes, working on legal cases through IRAP, in addition to co-organizing a charity clothing drive in order raise money for The Amal Foundation, as well as collect donations for the Middletown Resettlement Coalition. She is inspired for the Wesleyan group to continue to collaborate with organizations on both a campus-wide, and international level.

About Yael

Yael is a third-year student at Wesleyan University in the College of Social Studies, where she studies social theory, economic theory, political science, and history. As an Israeli-American, she has long been engaged in progressive advocacy and social justice work around the Israel-Palestine conflict and, in particular, how the idea of refugees connects to that region. This interest in advancing the rights of refugees led her to join the leadership of WRP’s advocacy team. She is excited to continue expanding the depth and breadth of how Wesleyan students can engage with refugee-related social justice, whether through assisting with resettlement in our Connecticut backyard or using an up-and-coming WRP data-base that corrals U.S legislation on refugee issues to call and write letters to local, state, and federal officials.

On campus, Yael is an active member of the Wesleyan Doula Project and J Street U Wesleyan, and choreographs community-based dances for the bi-yearly Terpsichore show. She hails from New York City, and hopes to return there some day and continue this work on a larger scale.
